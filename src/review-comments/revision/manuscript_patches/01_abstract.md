> Proactive identification of students who need academic support, combined with personalized intervention strategies, is essential for improving outcomes in higher education. This study proposes an end-to-end Explainable AI (XAI) framework that addresses three critical educational objectives simultaneously: (1) academic performance assessment—classifying students into Low, Medium, and High CGPA categories; (2) at-risk student identification—detecting students likely to struggle academically (CGPA below 7.0 or carrying backlogs); and (3) career readiness evaluation—assessing whether a student is prepared to start their professional career, measured through completion of internships and academic projects. Using a custom dataset of **1,378 engineering students** with **52 raw survey features** that, after preprocessing and engineering, yield **« N_FINAL » model-input features** spanning academic, demographic, behavioural, and socio-economic dimensions, we develop a feature-engineering pipeline that creates five composite indicators (`academic_index`, `skills_index`, `engagement_score`, `study_stress_interaction`, `skills_study_interaction`). Seven models—four machine learning (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost) and three deep learning (TabNet, FT-Transformer, SAINT)—are systematically compared using 5-fold stratified cross-validation with SMOTE-based class-imbalance handling and Optuna hyperparameter optimisation. XGBoost achieves the best performance with F1-macro of 0.805 (±0.017) for academic assessment, 0.767 (±0.040) for at-risk identification, and 0.699 (±0.024) for career readiness. SHAP analysis reveals that the engineered `academic_index` is the strongest predictor of academic outcomes, while `engagement_score` drives career readiness. A SHAP-based recommendation engine translates these insights into personalized, **hypothesis-generating** interventions for academic advisors—for example, workshop participation shows a SHAP impact of +0.331 for at-risk students. We also report a propensity-score-matched causal-plausibility check, subgroup fairness analysis (gender, residence, income), and a head-to-head comparison with three 2024–2025 SHAP-based EDM methods on the same dataset under an identical evaluation protocol. The framework provides educators and academic advisors with transparent, evidence-based decision-support tools.
